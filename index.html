A small box that carries a country home.

Root Crate is a curated box of flavors, objects, and stories from one country at a time. We start with Pakistan, and we have many more to come.

Our idea

Heritage, hand-picked.

Every Root Crate is built around a single country. Inside, you'll find the snacks people grew up on, the drinks shared at family tables, and a few quiet, surprising objects that tell you something about the place.

We're not trying to summarize a culture. We're trying to send you a thoughtful slice of one: to remind you of home, to introduce you to somewhere new, or just to give you something delightful to open.

Our first crate celebrates Pakistan. The next ones will travel elsewhere.

Inside Crate No 01

The Pakistan Crate.

Five staples chosen for nostalgia, plus three quiet surprises that speak to Pakistan in their own way. Each crate is small, deliberate, and built to be opened slowly.

The crimson syrup

Rooh Afza

A century-old rose-and-herb cordial poured over ice on summer afternoons. Pour, stir, remember.

Crunchy & fiery

Nimco Spicy Sticks

Crisp, fiery potato sticks dusted with chili and salt. The packet that disappears at every gathering.

Fruity jellies

Chili Mili

The rainbow-bright fruity chews of every Pakistani childhood. Tangy, sweet, and impossible to eat just one of.

Tea-time classic

Sooper

The original Pakistani biscuit. Crisp, faintly sweet, and best dipped twice into a steaming cup of milky chai.

The green soda

Pakola

The cult-classic ice-cream soda in electric green. A taste of every Karachi summer, in a single can.
